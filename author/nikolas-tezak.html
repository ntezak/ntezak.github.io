Welcome to my page

Hi, this is my site. There's a little bit about me here as well as some useful scripts/notebooks and just general notes in my blog.

About me

I'm originally from Leverkusen, in Germany. That's where the company Bayer was founded and Aspirin was patented.

I obtained my Physik Diplom from Heidelberg University in 2010, doing my thesis research on lattices of ultra-cold Rydbergatoms in the group of Peter Schmelcher, now at Hamburg University.

After some traveling within the US and to New Zealand I became a PhD student in Stanford University's Department of Applied Physics.

You can also find me on LinkedIn.

My research

My research is on photonic circuit models in the ultra-low-power/quantum regime and my PhD advisor is Hideo Mabuchi . My labmates and I spend our time investigating and characterizing indidividual circuit components such particular non-linear optical resonator models as well as things you can achieve by cascading these models in circuits.

Check out my Mendeley profile for a list of my publications.

Software I've written

I've written a couple of Python and Julia packages which can be found under my github account. The most important ones are:

  • QNET which is for my research and includes both symbolic algebra code implementing a quantum mechanical operator algebra as well as a quantum feedback circuit algebra which we use in our research.
  • Cirq is an IPython notebook widget which implements a graphical user interface for editing circuits from inside an IPython notebook. We use it for our research, but it is actually completely domain unspecific and can be interfaced with all sorts of circuit simulation backends.
